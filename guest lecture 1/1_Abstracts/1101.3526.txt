On the dynamics of non-reducible cylindrical vortices;;; D. Coronel,,  <a href="/find/math/1/au:+Navas_A/0/1/0/all/0/1">A. Navas</a>,  <a href="/find/math/1/au:+Ponce_M/0/1/0/all/0/1">M. Ponce</a> ;;; We study skew-maps given by a minimal homeomorphism on the basis and a cocycle of affine isometries on the fibers. We call such a map a cylindrical vortex. We extend to this setting some classical results of Atkinson, Besicovitch, Matsumoto-Shishikura and Schinelman (among other people) about cylindrical cascades. In particular, we show that no cylindrical vortex is minimal, and we construct interesting examples of topologically transitive ones.
