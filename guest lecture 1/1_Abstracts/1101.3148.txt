Biharmonic hypersurfaces in a Riemannian manifold with non-positive  Ricci curvature;;; Nobumitsu Nakauchi,,  <a href="/find/math/1/au:+Urakawa_H/0/1/0/all/0/1">Hajime Urakawa</a> ;;; In this paper, we show that, for a biharmonic hypersurface $(M,g)$ of a Riemannian manifold $(N,h)$ of non-positive Ricci curvature, if $\int_M|H|^2 v_g&lt;\infty$, where $H$ is the mean curvature of $(M,g)$ in $(N,h)$, then $(M,g)$ is minimal in $(N,h)$. Thus, for a counter example $(M,g)$ in the case of hypersurfaces to the generalized Chen's conjecture (cf. Sect.1), it holds that $\int_M|H|^2 v_g=\infty$.
